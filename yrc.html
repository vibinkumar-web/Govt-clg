About YRC

The Youth Red Cross (YRC) is the youth wing of the Indian Red Cross Society, operating actively in colleges and universities across India to instil humanitarian values among students. It operates under the principles of the International Red Cross — humanity, impartiality, neutrality, independence, voluntary service, unity, and universality.

The YRC unit at Government Arts and Science College, Sankarankovil aims to develop the spirit of voluntary service, health awareness, and social responsibility so that young people become active contributors to their communities. Through its activities, student volunteers build leadership skills, compassion, and a lifelong commitment to serving society without discrimination.

Key Activities

🩸

Blood Grouping & Donation

YRC organises blood grouping camps and donation drives in collaboration with government hospitals, encouraging students to become regular blood donors.

🩺

First Aid Training

Certified first aid training sessions are conducted for student volunteers, equipping them with life-saving skills for emergencies and disasters.

🏥

Health & Hygiene Awareness

Programmes on personal hygiene, safe drinking water, sanitation, and communicable disease prevention are conducted in schools and villages.

⛑

Relief Activities

YRC volunteers participate in relief and rehabilitation work during floods, droughts, and other natural calamities alongside district administration.

👤

Eye & Health Camps

Free medical and eye check-up camps are organised for rural communities and college students in partnership with government health departments.

🏻

Thalassaemia & AIDS Awareness

Special awareness campaigns on thalassaemia, HIV/AIDS, and other public health issues are conducted to reduce stigma and spread knowledge.

YRC Warden

The YRC unit is coordinated by a faculty warden appointed by the college Principal. The warden oversees volunteer enrolment, activity planning, and maintains liaison with the Indian Red Cross Society, Tamil Nadu State Branch.

Active YRC members who complete the prescribed service hours and training receive certificates from the Indian Red Cross Society, which are valuable for government and healthcare sector employment.
