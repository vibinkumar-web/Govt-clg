About NSS

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a Central Government initiative under the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, launched in 1969 during the birth-centenary year of Mahatma Gandhi. Its motto, “Not Me But You,” reflects its core aim of developing the personality and character of student volunteers through community service.

The NSS unit at Government Arts and Science College, Sankarankovil actively mobilises students to engage in social welfare work in the surrounding communities of Tenkasi district. Volunteers contribute to nation-building activities while developing leadership skills, social responsibility, and a spirit of selfless service.

Key Activities

🥀

Blood Donation Drives

Regular blood donation camps are organised in association with government hospitals to meet the blood requirements of patients in Tenkasi district.

🌿

Environmental Initiatives

Tree plantation drives, cleanliness campaigns, and plastic-free awareness programmes are conducted on campus and in nearby villages.

📚

Literacy Campaigns

NSS volunteers conduct literacy and awareness programmes in rural areas, supporting adult education and voter awareness drives.

🏠

Annual Special Camp

Every year, NSS volunteers adopt a nearby village for 7 days to carry out social welfare activities — health camps, sanitation work, and community development.

🏥

Disaster Relief

NSS volunteers are trained to assist during natural calamities and relief operations, working with district administration and NGOs.

👥

Social Awareness

Programmes on gender equality, anti-drug campaigns, road safety, and digital literacy are conducted to raise awareness among students and communities.

NSS Programme Officer

The NSS unit at Government Arts and Science College, Sankarankovil is guided by a dedicated Programme Officer appointed by the college. The officer coordinates all NSS activities, maintains records of volunteer service hours, and liaises with the NSS regional centre and Manonmaniam Sundaranar University NSS cell.

Students who complete 240 hours of NSS service over two years are awarded the NSS certificate by Manonmaniam Sundaranar University, which is recognised by central and state government recruitment boards.
