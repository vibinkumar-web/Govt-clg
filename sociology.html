About the Department

The Department of Sociology offers the B.A. Sociology programme, a three-year undergraduate course affiliated to Manonmaniam Sundaranar University, Tirunelveli. The department explores the dynamics of human society, social structures, culture, and change, equipping students to understand and address real-world social issues.

The programme emphasises social theory, research methodology, and field studies. Students gain insights into community development, social welfare, gender studies, tribal studies, and social movements. Practical field work and community-based projects are integral to the curriculum, enabling students to connect classroom learning with grassroots social realities.

Graduates are well-prepared for careers in social work, government services, NGOs, community development, journalism, education, and research. Many students also pursue postgraduate studies in M.A. Sociology, MSW, and civil services preparation.

Head of the Department

Dr. N. Sukumaran

Assistant Professor & Head — Department of Sociology

Dr. N. Sukumaran heads the Department of Sociology with deep expertise in social theory, community development, and sociological research. His scholarly work and commitment to student welfare have made the department a centre of learning and social awareness.

Under his guidance, students have conducted meaningful field studies in the Tenkasi district, contributing to community-based research and social welfare activities. He actively mentors students for civil services and higher education opportunities.

Syllabus — B.A. Sociology

The syllabus is prescribed by Manonmaniam Sundaranar University and is subject to revision as per UGC and university norms. The programme spans six semesters.

Semester I & II — Foundation

  • Introduction to Sociology
  • Social Institutions — Family, Marriage, Kinship
  • Tamil / Other Language (Part I)
  • English Communication Skills (Part II)
  • Environmental Studies

Semester III & IV — Core Studies

  • Sociological Theory — Classical and Modern
  • Social Stratification — Caste, Class, Gender
  • Indian Society and Social Change
  • Research Methods in Social Sciences
  • Allied: Political Science / Social Psychology

Semester V & VI — Advanced & Electives

  • Rural and Urban Sociology
  • Sociology of Development
  • Tribal Studies and Marginalized Communities
  • Social Welfare and Social Work
  • Elective: Gender Studies / Environmental Sociology / Industrial Sociology
  • Field Work Project & Viva Voce

For the complete and updated syllabus, refer to the Manonmaniam Sundaranar University official syllabus document or contact the department office.
